Drill: Jackalling Circuit
Category: Breakdown / Contact Skills
Duration: 20 minutes
Age Group: Senior / U18
Equipment: 4 tackle bags, 4 shields, 8 cones, 1 ball per group

OBJECTIVE
Develop legal jackalling technique — arriving at the breakdown low and early with a stable body position to contest the ball.

SETUP
Create a 20m x 20m grid. Set four breakdown stations (cones marking rucks) spaced around the perimeter. Place a tackle bag next to each station.

EXECUTION

Phase 1 — Technique Walk-Through (5 min)
Coach demonstrates three jackal entry positions:
  1. Arriving from the side with low hips
  2. Stepping over the ball with feet straddled
  3. Hands reaching through under the ball carrier's chest

Players observe then practise static entry in pairs with no resistance.

Phase 2 — Individual Reps (8 min)
Players queue at station 1. Ball carrier goes to ground holding ball. Jackaller drives forward, hips low, body over ball, and contests for 3 seconds. Defenders (shields) apply light pressure on the jackaller's back from the correct gate.
Switch roles after 4 reps.

Progression: Add a competing cleanout player who attempts to legally remove the jackaller. Jackaller must maintain legal body position.

Phase 3 — Live Competition (7 min)
Two teams of 5. Attack carries ball into contact from set piece. Defence competes to contest at breakdown. Referee (coach or player) awards jackalling turnover or team ball. First to 5 turnovers wins.

COACHING POINTS
- Entry must be from the direction of travel — arriving through the gate
- Hips below shoulder height at all times
- Head and neck not in a dangerous position
- Feet planted — no going to ground unless the ball carrier is down first
- Legal bind if competing over the ball (hands through, not grabbing)

SAFETY NOTE
Coaches should not allow players to compete at the breakdown until they have demonstrated correct body position in Phase 1. Stop and correct any high entry immediately.
