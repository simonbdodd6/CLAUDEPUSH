World Rugby Law Amendment — Tackle Height Directive
Effective: 1 January 2024

SUMMARY FOR COACHES AND REFEREES

World Rugby has implemented a new directive on tackle height across all levels of the game. The directive lowers the legal tackle height and introduces clearer guidance on what constitutes dangerous play.

KEY CHANGES:

1. TACKLE HEIGHT
The legal tackle height is now defined as at or below the line of the hips. Tackles that make initial contact above this line — even if the tackler brings the ball carrier down safely — are to be treated as potential dangerous play.

2. HHIA (Head and High Impact Assessment)
Any player who receives a head or high impact in a tackle must be assessed using the HHIA protocol. Coaches are required to understand and support this process, including removing a player from play if the assessment indicates possible concussion.

3. SANCTIONS
- Tackles above the hip with no injury: Yellow card
- Tackles above the hip resulting in head contact: Red card (no mitigation for accidental)

COACHING IMPLICATIONS

Coaches must immediately:
a) Review tackle technique in every training session. The emphasis must be on the tackler's body position and entry height, not just the outcome of the contact.
b) Teach players to target the ball carrier's midriff or below as the primary contact zone.
c) Introduce the "low entry" position: hips low, back flat, head to the side (cheek to cheek) before contact.

The welfare of all players — particularly youth players — depends on coaches prioritising correct technique over power or dominance in the tackle.

SAFETY NOTE:
Players who demonstrate repeated high tackles should be removed from contact practice until their technique is corrected. This is a welfare requirement, not a coaching preference.

Reference: World Rugby Law 10.4(a) and Regulation 17
