Coaching note — Session debrief 8 June 2024

Had a great session with the U16s this evening. Focused on the lineout after last week's issues at the maul.

Key things I noticed:
- The jumpers are not communicating the call clearly enough — the hooker is throwing before the jumper is set
- Front lifter keeps coming up too early which is causing the jump to be off-balance
- The call needs to come from the number 8, not the 4 or 5

What worked well: the walking lineout drills we tried at the start. Walking it through at half speed really helped them understand the timing.

Next session plan:
1. Start with the static lineout drill — no throw, just practice the lift and jump technique
2. Add a soft throw once the technique is solid
3. Finish with live lineouts under pressure (3 attempts each end)

Note to self: check the World Rugby youth lineout laws — there are specific rules about lifting in age-grade rugby that I need to review before next training.

Also worth exploring: the maul from the lineout. We're losing the maul drive too quickly. Players are releasing the bind too early. Need a drill that specifically works on maintaining the bind under pressure.

Parent mentioned one of the players has a shoulder issue from last week — need to check with parents before letting him do contact. Welfare check before Thursday session.
