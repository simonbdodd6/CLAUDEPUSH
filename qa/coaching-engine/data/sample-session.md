
# Training Session — breakdown and rucking technique — U16

*U16 | 90min | Intensity: high | Provider: template*

---


## Warm-Up (14min)


### Dynamic warm-up with ball

*Duration: 7min*
Players in pairs with a ball. Easy passing jog, gradually increasing distance and pace. Add skip turns, high knees, and side shuffles.


### Coaching Points

- Ball in two hands at all times
- Eyes up — scan the space
- Communicate with your partner

### Competitive handling warm-up

*Duration: 7min*
Small-sided passing game (3v3 or 4v4) — keep ball alive, continuous movement. 10×10m grid.


### Coaching Points

- Catch first, then run
- Support player — always be within pass distance

---


## Skill Block 1 — breakdown and rucking technique (technique) (23min)


### breakdown and rucking technique — unopposed technical drill

*Setup: 4 cones in a 10×10m box. Groups of 4–6 players.*

Introduce the skill at walk pace → jog pace. Correct technique before adding speed. Use coaching questions: "What should your body be doing here?" Coach circles the group giving individual feedback.


### Coaching Points

- Technique is non-negotiable — no shortcuts to keep pace
- Reset after every mistake — build good habit
- Key insight from knowledge base: EXECUTION Phase 1 — Technique Walk-Through (5 min) Coach demonstrates three jackal entry positions:  1.

### Progressions

- Add a second player
- Add time pressure
- Add a defender in passive role

### Safety

- Contact drills — check equipment
- No contact until technique is established in unopposed phase

---


## Skill Block 2 — breakdown and rucking technique (competitive application) (23min)


### breakdown and rucking technique — conditioned game

*Setup: 20×15m grid. Two teams. Score points for executing the skill correctly.*

Small-sided game with a condition that rewards correct execution of the session skill. Example: a try only counts if the scoring sequence included the session skill.


### Coaching Points

- The skill must appear under competitive pressure today
- Award bonus points for skill execution — make it worth doing correctly
- Stop and reset if the skill breaks down — reinforce the technique before restarting

### Progressions

- Remove the condition
- Add a second condition
- Increase grid size

### Safety

- full contact — contested scrums and lineouts introduced

---


## Conditioning (9min)

Interval runs (3×5min at threshold pace) or shuttle conditioning (10×30m with 20s rest)

---


## Cool-Down (7min)

- Light jog to bring heart rate down (2min)
- Static stretching: hip flexors, hamstrings, calves (30s each)
- Team debrief — 3 things we did well, 1 we need to improve
- Hydration reminder and next session information

---


## Equipment Needed

- Rugby balls (1 per 2 players)
- Cones (20+)
- Bibs (two colours)
- Tackle bags (4)

---


## Overall Coaching Points

- breakdown and rucking technique is the theme — every activity should reinforce it
- Contested set-piece is new — technique and safety must be coached explicitly every session.
- Positive coaching environment — celebrate effort, correct with purpose
- Give every player equal time with the ball

---


## Safety Notes

- full contact — contested scrums and lineouts introduced
- U16 session duration: 90 minutes — do not overrun
- Ensure adequate water breaks — every 20 minutes minimum
- Injury incident protocol: any player showing signs of concussion leaves the field immediately
